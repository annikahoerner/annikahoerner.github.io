## Analyzing uncertainty & error in Malcomb et al (2014)
### Using Tate (2013) framework for how to read error


# localizing social vulnerability 
Tate emphasizes the importance of extensive qualitative research that provides the basis for the vulnerability index. In Malcomb et al (2014), the process of determining his weighting schematic through his discussions with "experts" is not completely explored in the final paper. While some of these expert interviews are recorded, there remains an unseen sector that opens the door for further uncertainty. This might fit in with what Tate defines as "epistemic uncertainty," where the data is emerging from irreproducible sources. Without clarity on how these interviewees were selected / where their expertise derives from, there is uncertainty and a potential skew in the study design, particularly in determining the weighting schematic. 


# data accessibility 
Another aspect of the Malcomb study that leaves it open for issues of uncertainty is the issues of accesibility with the data. We have discussed how cleaning and organizing this data is an extensive manual process; this opens the door for "aleatoric data". 


# descriptive analysis & interpretation
Particularly when navigating such complex and nebulous topics like spatial vulnerability and climate adapativity, defining these terms early on is critical to the interpretation and communciation of results. This paper does not explicitly define these terms, and therefore opens avenues for uncertainty in interpreting the results.
