# How does disability interact with Covid-19 incidence?
## Reproducing Chakraborty's study

This most recent update of the Chakraborty reproduction includes additional maps for vizualizing and interpreting the results of the GEE models. 

Working through this reproduction highlighted how many choices go into completing a study, which makes its reproduction even more difficult if those choices are not articulated clearly. I was surprised to see that there were discrepancies bewteen the reproduced analysis--both in the data management (those counties whose data was switched) and in the programming and in the ultimate results. 

This process also showed the role of step-by-step validation throughout the study to identify and isolate the sources of error. For example, it was critical to calculate and compare the summary statistics about the dataset right away to ensure that both the original study and its reproduction were emerging from the same source data. 

The reproduction follows a similar analytical framework, but it ultimately expands on the original study in illuminating the analytical steps that it requires. Visualizing the steps of the statistical analyses is powerful in illuminating what is often "blackboxed" by proprietary software. In this sense, the process of engaging in the reproduction and improving the study in its clarity ultimately furthers the understanding of the statistical tests that underwrite the analysis. 

I am interested in seeing how this study holds up when replicated across another era of the pandemic; will the results and coefficients derived from this first chunk of data hold true over the following months of the pandemic?
